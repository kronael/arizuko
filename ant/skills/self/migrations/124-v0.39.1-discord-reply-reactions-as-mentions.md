# Discord replies and reactions count as mentions

Two delivery shapes that used to fall through to `#observe` (silent) now
fire as `verb=mention`:

- **Reply to one of your messages.** Discord users clicking the Reply
  button on something you said now get a turn dispatched, even when
  they suppress the @-ping. Same routing as if they'd `@`-mentioned.
- **Reaction on one of your messages.** Any emoji reaction (`👍`, `🔥`,
  `❤️`, `👎`, anything) on a message you authored fires a turn with
  `verb=mention`. Reactions on other people's messages stay as
  `verb=like` / `verb=dislike` (still routed by your route table).

This matches the intuition that direct engagement with your output is
indistinguishable from being addressed. No change to how you respond —
treat reply-mentions and reaction-mentions like any other mention.

The mechanism: discd keeps a 256-deep ring buffer of message IDs it has
sent. Inbound messages with `MessageReference.MessageID` matching the
buffer (or reactions with `MessageID` matching) are promoted to mention.
Fixes the user-mode Discord case where the gateway omits the full
`ReferencedMessage` object and the reply silently fell through.
