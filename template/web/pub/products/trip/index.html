Trip planner (May)

State the brief once. May researches, synthesises, and delivers a day-by-day itinerary with logistics, visa, budget, and packing. Preferences persist across every future trip.

A single-user agent tied to your travel profile and past trips. Not a search engine — it reads your preferences first, clarifies before researching, and produces a structured document, not a list of links. Template at ant/examples/trip/.

persona

May is methodical and precise. She cites sources inline (“per the Japan visa gov page” or “per Lonely Planet §Kyoto”). She asks clarifying questions before researching — dates, budget, travel style — and never starts building the plan until those are settled. She won’t invent visa requirements, prices, or opening hours; she verifies or says she’s uncertain. Delivers structured documents: day-by-day schedule, logistics block, budget estimate, packing list.

system shape

  • Brief intake + clarify. The agent reads your brief, checks facts/preferences.md for your travel profile, and asks any missing questions before committing to research. Dates, budget range, pace, dietary restrictions — once settled, research starts.
  • Preferences file. facts/preferences.md is the traveller profile: typical budget range, accommodation style, pace (relaxed vs packed), dietary restrictions, interests. Populated by the operator before deploy; updated by the agent over time as you mention preferences in conversation.
  • Multi-step research via oracle. For each destination May runs a multi-step synthesis: web search for current conditions, visa requirements, transport, and accommodation; oracle synthesis across the gathered sources into a coherent plan. Oracle is required — this product won’t produce good output without CODEX_API_KEY.
  • Structured day-by-day output. The itinerary lands as a structured document: day-by-day schedule, logistics (flights, ground transport), visa block, budget estimate, packing list. Saved to facts/trips/YYYY-<dest>.md after delivery.
  • Trip archive. Every completed trip is stored at facts/trips/YYYY-<dest>.md. “How did the Kyoto trip go?” pulls from the archive.
  • Published itinerary (optional). The agent can write a formatted HTML version to ~/public_html/ so you can share a link with travel companions without giving them chat access.

example: brief to confirm-research

telegram · DM

  user   10 days in Japan, late October, food focus, mid-budget
  may    Got it — a few questions before I start:
         1. Rough budget per day (not counting flights)?
         2. Accommodation style: hostel, business hotel, ryokan, or mix?
         3. Any dietary restrictions I should route around?
         4. Flying into Tokyo or open to landing elsewhere?

  user   ~¥15k/day, mix of business hotel + one ryokan, no restrictions,
         fine to fly into Tokyo
  may    Perfect. I'll research: Tokyo (3d), Hakone (1d), Kyoto (3d),
         Osaka (2d), day-trip to Nara. Pulling current visa rules,
         Oct rail passes, and food spots. Back in a few minutes.

  [May runs web search + oracle synthesis]

  may    Here's the plan — 10 days, food-first, mid-budget. Sending
         as a file.

  [sends facts/trips/2026-japan.md]

         Summary: Tokyo 3 nights (Shinjuku base, Tsukiji + Yanaka markets,
         ramen crawl), Hakone overnight ryokan (Fuji view, kaiseki dinner),
         Kyoto 3 nights (Nishiki market, Fushimi Inari at dawn, Gion dinner),
         Osaka 2 nights (Dotonbori, Kuromon market, takoyaki circuit),
         Nara half-day. Budget est. ~¥142k total (ex flights).

required skills

  • web — required. Live search for visa rules, transport, current prices.
  • oracle — required. Multi-step synthesis across gathered sources. Needs CODEX_API_KEY.
  • facts, recall-memories, find — required. Reads preferences; looks up past trips.
  • diary — required. Logs each trip research session for future recall.

channels

Telegram is the primary channel — personal, ongoing, and the natural fit for a single-user planner. WhatsApp is the alternative for users who don’t use Telegram. The same asymmetric-channel pattern used by the reality agent applies here: if you want WhatsApp for ingest but Telegram as the reply channel, route WhatsApp to #observe and let May surface relevant messages in the Telegram thread. See the reality agent case study for the exact route-table shape.

data & trust

  • Your server. Preferences, trip archive, diary, and conversation history — all under /srv/data/arizuko_<name>/. Backup is one tar; no DB dump.
  • Default outbound paths. Telegram for bot traffic; Anthropic for model calls; oracle via CODEX_API_KEY. Any web research egress goes through crackbox — you allowlist the domains May can reach.
  • Single user. This is a personal deployment. There are no per-user memory files because there’s only one user. The traveller profile is the whole of facts/preferences.md.

setup

arizuko create travel --product trip
cd /srv/data/arizuko_travel
$EDITOR .env             # TELEGRAM_BOT_TOKEN, CODEX_API_KEY (required), ANTHROPIC_API_KEY
$EDITOR groups/may/facts/preferences.md  # seed traveller profile
arizuko run travel

Full step-by-step is in the operator setup guide — seeding the preferences file, enabling the oracle skill, and verifying the first research run.
